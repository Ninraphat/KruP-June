The School Science Fair

~160 words · Narrative · B1

Every year, the science committee organises a big fair at our school. Each student prepares a project and presents it to the judges. This year, the number of participants has reached two hundred.

Mathematics and physics are the most popular topics. However, economics is also gaining attention this year. Neither the teachers nor the principal was expecting such a large turnout.

The audience was impressed by every display. The scissors and glue were left on the table after the fair. Everyone agrees that the news about this year's fair is truly inspiring. The majority of students plans to participate again next year.

Q

Reading Comprehension

Click an option to check your answer instantly.

1 Who organises the science fair each year?
A The students.
B The science committee.
C The principal.
D The audience.
✅ "The science committee organises…" — committee is a collective noun treated as singular → organises.
❌ Re-read paragraph 1. Who does the organising?
2 Which word in the passage is an "always singular" noun even though it ends in -s?
A scissors
B participants
C economics
D teachers
✅ "Economics is also gaining attention." Subjects ending in -ics (economics, mathematics, physics, politics) always take a singular verb.
❌ Look for a word ending in -ics that uses "is" in the passage.
3 "Neither the teachers nor the principal was expecting…" — Why is was correct here?
A Because teachers is the subject.
B Because the verb agrees with the noun closest to it (the principal = singular).
C Because neither…nor always uses singular verb.
D Because principal is plural.
✅ With neither…nor and either…or, the verb agrees with the noun CLOSEST to it. "…nor the principal" → singular → was.
❌ The rule for neither…nor is the "proximity rule" — the verb matches the NEAREST subject.

Section C — The 12 Agreement Rules

Click each rule to expand it. Every rule includes examples and Thai explanations.

🔑
The Golden Rule: A singular subject takes a singular verb (adds -s/-es in Present Simple). A plural subject takes a plural verb (no -s).
ประธานเอกพจน์ → กริยาเติม -s  ·  ประธานพหูพจน์ → กริยาไม่เติม -s

✦ Singular Subject → Verb + s

She runs every day.
The dog barks at night.
He does his homework.

✦ Plural Subject → Verb (no s)

They run every day.
The dogs bark at night.
They do their homework.
1
Subjects joined by and → Plural verb
plural ›
📌
When two subjects are joined by and, the combined subject is plural and takes a plural verb.
ประธานสองตัวเชื่อมด้วย and → ถือเป็นพหูพจน์
✅
Tom and Anna are best friends.
✅
The cat and the dog play together.
❌
Tom and Anna is best friends.Wrong — two subjects joined by "and" = plural.
⚠️
Exception: When two nouns refer to the same person/thing, use singular: "The director and founder is arriving." (one person holds both roles)
2
Either…or / Neither…nor → Proximity Rule
proximity ›
📌
With either…or and neither…nor, the verb agrees with the subject closest to it (the one right before the verb).
กริยาตามประธานที่อยู่ใกล้ที่สุด (ตัวที่สอง)
✅
Either she or they are right. (closest: they = plural)
✅
Neither the boys nor the teacher was late. (closest: teacher = singular)
✅
Either you or he is responsible. (closest: he = singular)
3
Collective nouns (team, class, family…) → Usually singular
singular ›
📌
Collective nouns refer to a group acting as one unit → singular verb. Common ones: team, class, family, committee, jury, audience, government, staff, crowd, group, club, flock, army
คำนามหมู่คณะ → ถือเป็นเอกพจน์ในภาษาอังกฤษแบบอเมริกัน
✅
The team plays well together.
✅
The committee has made its decision.
✅
My family lives in Chiang Mai.
❌
The team play well together.Wrong in American English — collective nouns take singular verbs.
4
Subjects ending in -ics → Always singular
always singular ›
📌
Subjects of study or fields ending in -ics always take a singular verb, even though they look plural.
วิชาเรียนที่ลงท้าย -ics → เสมอใช้กริยาเอกพจน์
SubjectCorrect VerbThai
Mathematicsis / was / requiresคณิตศาสตร์
Physicsis / was / explainsฟิสิกส์
Economicsis / was / studiesเศรษฐศาสตร์
Politicsis / was / influencesรัฐศาสตร์ / การเมือง
Statisticsis / was / showsสถิติ
Linguisticsis / was / studiesภาษาศาสตร์
✅
Mathematics is a compulsory subject.
❌
Mathematics are compulsory.Wrong — -ics subjects always use singular verb.
5
Uncountable nouns → Always singular
always singular ›
📌
Uncountable nouns (things we cannot count individually) always take singular verbs. They have no plural form.
คำนามนับไม่ได้ → ใช้กริยาเอกพจน์เสมอ ไม่มีรูปพหูพจน์
Uncountable NounThaiExample
newsข่าวThe news is good.
adviceคำแนะนำYour advice is helpful.
informationข้อมูลThe information is correct.
furnitureเฟอร์นิเจอร์The furniture looks new.
luggageกระเป๋าเดินทางHer luggage was lost.
knowledgeความรู้Knowledge is power.
trafficการจราจรThe traffic is bad today.
6
Nouns always plural (no singular form) → Plural verb
always plural ›
📌
Some nouns exist only in plural form (usually pairs/sets). They always take plural verbs and use "a pair of" to specify one item.
คำนามที่มีเฉพาะรูปพหูพจน์ → ใช้กริยาพหูพจน์เสมอ
NounThaiCorrect Usage
scissorsกรรไกรThe scissors are sharp. / A pair of scissors is on the table.
trousersกางเกงขายาวThese trousers are too long.
glassesแว่นตาHis glasses were broken.
policeตำรวจThe police are here.
peopleผู้คนPeople are kind here.
cattleวัวควายThe cattle graze freely.
7
Each / Every / Everyone / Someone / No one… → Singular
singular ›
📌
Indefinite pronouns and determiners that describe individuals always take singular verbs.
คำที่บ่งบอกถึงแต่ละบุคคล → ใช้กริยาเอกพจน์
WordThaiExample
each / everyแต่ละ / ทุกๆEach student has a textbook.
everyone / everybodyทุกคนEveryone is here.
someone / somebodyบางคนSomeone left a bag.
no one / nobodyไม่มีใครNobody knows the answer.
anyone / anybodyใครก็ตามAnyone is welcome.
everything / nothingทุกสิ่ง / ไม่มีอะไรEverything looks fine.
❌
Everyone have finished.Wrong — "everyone" is always singular → "Everyone has finished."
8
"The number of" vs "A number of"
tricky pair ›
⚡
This is one of the most common exam traps! The two phrases mean different things:
คู่นี้เป็นกับดักข้อสอบยอดนิยม!

"THE number of" → singular

The number of students is large.
"The number" = the amount itself (1 thing)

"A number of" → plural

A number of students are absent.
"A number of" = several / many (plural meaning)
🧠
Memory trick: "THE number" → singular (the one specific number). "A number of" → many (plural). Think: "many students are absent."
9
Subject with "of" phrase — ignore the middle part
proximity trap ›
📌
When the subject is followed by a prepositional phrase (of the…), the verb agrees with the main subject, not the noun inside the "of" phrase.
กริยาตามประธานหลัก ไม่ใช่คำนามใน of phrase
✅
The box of chocolates is on the table.Subject = "the box" (singular) → is
✅
The members of the club meet on Fridays.Subject = "the members" (plural) → meet
❌
The box of chocolates are on the table.Wrong — "chocolates" is NOT the subject. "Box" (singular) is.
10
Gerund or Infinitive as subject → Singular
singular ›
📌
When a verb form (gerund = V-ing, or infinitive = to + V) acts as the subject, it is treated as a singular noun.
กริยาที่ทำหน้าที่เป็นประธาน (gerund/infinitive) → ถือเป็นเอกพจน์
✅
Swimming is great exercise.
✅
Learning English takes time and practice.
✅
To win the competition is her dream.
❌
Running are good for health.
11
Both…and vs Not only…but also
conjunctions ›
ConjunctionVerb RuleExample
both … andPluralBoth Tom and Lisa are here.
not only … but alsoProximityNot only the teacher but also the students are tired.
as well asAgrees with 1st SThe teacher, as well as the students, is tired.
together withAgrees with 1st SThe manager, together with his team, was present.
along withAgrees with 1st SThe coach, along with the players, is on the bus.
💡
Key tip: When a phrase like as well as, together with, or along with comes between the subject and verb, it is like adding extra information in brackets — ignore it and focus on the MAIN subject before it.
12
There is / There are — Verb agrees with the following noun
there sentences ›
📌
In sentences starting with There, the verb agrees with the noun that comes AFTER it (the real subject), not with "there".
ประโยคที่ขึ้นต้นด้วย There → กริยาตามคำนามที่ตามหลัง
✅
There is a cat in the garden. (a cat = singular)
✅
There are three books on the shelf. (three books = plural)
❌
There are a student waiting.Wrong — "a student" is singular → "There is a student waiting."

Section E — Unit Review

Quick-reference summary of all 12 rules + self-evaluation checklist.

Always Singular ✦

  • Collective nouns (team, family, committee…)
  • -ics subjects (math, physics, economics…)
  • Uncountable nouns (news, advice, info…)
  • each / every / everyone / nobody…
  • Gerund or infinitive as subject
  • "The number of" + noun

Always Plural ✦

  • Pair nouns (scissors, glasses, trousers…)
  • police / people / cattle
  • Both…and + two subjects
  • "A number of" + noun
  • Two subjects joined by and

Proximity Rule ✦

  • Either…or → verb matches closest noun
  • Neither…nor → verb matches closest noun
  • Not only…but also → verb matches closest noun

Ignore the Middle ✦

  • "Of phrase" — ignore, find main subject
  • "As well as" — ignore, use 1st subject
  • "Together with" — ignore, use 1st subject
  • "Along with" — ignore, use 1st subject
  • There is/are → check noun after verb
🚨
Top 6 Exam Traps (ข้อสอบชอบออก):
  1. The news are… ✗ → The news is… ✓ (uncountable)
  2. Mathematics are… ✗ → Mathematics is… ✓ (-ics = singular)
  3. The scissors was… ✗ → The scissors were… ✓ (always plural)
  4. Everyone have… ✗ → Everyone has… ✓ (always singular)
  5. The team are… ✗ → The team is… ✓ (collective = singular)
  6. A number of students is… ✗ → A number of students are… ✓ (plural)

✅ Self-Evaluation — "I Can…"

  • State the Golden Rule of subject–verb agreement.
  • Apply the proximity rule with either…or and neither…nor correctly.
  • Use singular verbs with collective nouns (team, committee, audience).
  • Identify -ics subjects and use singular verbs with them.
  • Use singular verbs with uncountable nouns (news, advice, information, furniture).
  • Use plural verbs with pair nouns (scissors, glasses, police, people).
  • Use singular verbs with each, every, everyone, nobody, anyone.
  • Distinguish between "the number of" (singular) and "a number of" (plural).
  • Ignore "of phrase / as well as / together with" and find the true subject.
  • Use singular verbs when a gerund or infinitive phrase is the subject.
  • Correctly complete a 10-blank SVA cloze passage.
  • Score at least 8/10 on the exam-style MCQ section.
