Ancient City Discovered in Thailand

~170 words · News Article · B1

An ancient city has been discovered deep in the forests of northern Thailand. The site was found by a team of archaeologists last month and is believed to be over 1,000 years old.

Hundreds of artefacts have been collected from the site. The objects are currently being examined by experts at Chiang Mai University. It is expected that many more discoveries will be made over the coming months.

The ancient city is thought to have been a major trading centre. Several gold coins were found near the entrance, and a large temple has been located at the centre of the site. The temple walls are decorated with beautiful carvings.

The Thai government has announced that the area will be protected as a national heritage site. Tourists will be allowed to visit once the initial research has been completed.

✓ "are being + V3 (examined)" = Present Continuous Passive. "Currently" signals an action happening right now.
✗ The formula "am/is/are + being + V3" = Present Continuous Passive.

Section C — Grammar Spotlight

Active vs Passive, when to use passive, and all 10 passive forms.

💡
What is Passive Voice?
In active voice, the subject DOES the action: "The dog bit the man."
In passive voice, the subject RECEIVES the action: "The man was bitten by the dog."
Passive voice = S (receiver) + be + V3 (+ by + agent)

When Do We Use Passive Voice?

SituationWhy passive?Example
🌍 The doer is unknownWe don't know who did it"My car was stolen last night."
📋 The doer is obvious / unimportantEveryone knows who; focus on result"Rice is grown in Thailand."
📰 Formal / academic / news writingRemoves personal subject for objectivity"It has been decided that..."
🎯 Focus on the object, not the doerThe receiver is more important"Ten people were injured in the accident."

The Golden Formula

Subject  +  be (correct tense)  +  V3 (past participle)  +  (by + agent)
⚡
The "by + agent" phrase is optional. Omit it when the doer is unknown, obvious, or unimportant.
"English is spoken here." (by people — too obvious to mention)

All 10 Passive Tense Forms

#TenseActive FormulaPassive FormulaExample (Passive)
1Present SimpleS + V1(s)S + am/is/are + V3This car is made in Germany.
2Present ContinuousS + am/is/are + V-ingS + am/is/are + being + V3The road is being repaired.
3Present PerfectS + have/has + V3S + have/has + been + V3The form has been signed.
4Past SimpleS + V2S + was/were + V3The window was broken.
5Past ContinuousS + was/were + V-ingS + was/were + being + V3The suspect was being questioned.
6Past PerfectS + had + V3S + had + been + V3The letter had been sent already.
7Future SimpleS + will + V1S + will + be + V3The prize will be announced tomorrow.
8Future PerfectS + will have + V3S + will have + been + V3The project will have been completed by June.
9Modal VerbsS + modal + V1S + modal + be + V3This rule must be followed.
10Modal PerfectS + modal + have + V3S + modal + have + been + V3It should have been done earlier.

How to Transform: Active → Passive (4 Steps)

StepWhat to doExample
Step 1Move the Object to Subject position"the book" becomes the new subject
Step 2Add the correct form of BE (match the tense)was / is / has been / will be...
Step 3Change the main verb to V3 (past participle)"wrote" → "written"
Step 4Add "by + original subject" (optional)"by Maya" (or omit if unimportant)
📋
Worked Example:
Active: "Maya writes beautiful poems."
Step 1: Object "beautiful poems" → new subject
Step 2: Present Simple → be = are
Step 3: "writes" → written
Step 4: by Maya (optional)
Passive: "Beautiful poems are written by Maya."

Common Errors

❌
Error 1 — Using V2 instead of V3:
"The book was wrote by Maya." ✗  →  "The book was written by Maya." ✓

Error 2 — Wrong form of BE:
"The letters was sent yesterday." ✗  →  "The letters were sent yesterday." ✓

Error 3 — Keeping the original subject in subject position:
"The meal was cooked by was delicious." ✗  →  Remove or restructure.

Error 4 — Using "by" when doer is unknown/obvious:
"Rice is grown by farmers in Thailand." → "Rice is grown in Thailand." (simpler and more natural)

Special Form: Impersonal Passive

🎯
Used when reporting what "people say / think / believe / know / expect / report."

Pattern 1: It + passive + that + clause
"It is believed that the city is 1,000 years old."

Pattern 2: Subject + passive + to + infinitive
"The city is believed to be 1,000 years old."

Common verbs: say, think, believe, know, expect, report, consider, claim, suppose

Five activities — from identifying passive voice to full exam-format practice.

1

Identify & Choose

Choose the correct passive verb form for each sentence.

📌 Choose the best answer. Each question tests a different passive tense form.
1 The Eiffel Tower _____ in Paris. (general fact)
A was located
B is located
C has been located
D is being located
✓ Present Simple Passive "is located" — general fact, permanent situation. Formula: S + is + V3.
✗ General facts use Present Simple Passive: S + am/is/are + V3.
2 The new hospital _____ at the moment. (action in progress now)
A is built
B is being built
C has been built
D was built
✓ Present Continuous Passive "is being built" — ongoing now. Formula: S + is/are + being + V3.
✗ "At the moment" = ongoing = Present Continuous Passive: am/is/are + being + V3.
3 The report _____ to the manager yet. (not yet, recent past with present relevance)
A wasn't sent
B hasn't been sent
C isn't being sent
D won't be sent
✓ Present Perfect Passive (negative) "hasn't been sent" — "yet" signals Present Perfect. Formula: haven't/hasn't + been + V3.
✗ "Yet" signals Present Perfect Passive: have/has + been + V3 (negative: haven't/hasn't been + V3).
4 Three people _____ in the accident yesterday.
A were injured
B are injured
C have been injured
D will be injured
✓ Past Simple Passive "were injured" — "yesterday" = specific past time. Plural subject "three people" → were. Formula: S + were + V3.
✗ "Yesterday" = specific past time = Past Simple Passive: S + was/were + V3.
5 The suspect _____ when the police arrived. (ongoing action interrupted)
A was questioned
B was being questioned
C had been questioned
D has been questioned
✓ Past Continuous Passive "was being questioned" — an ongoing past action ("when police arrived" = interruption). Formula: S + was/were + being + V3.
✗ An ongoing past action interrupted by another event = Past Continuous Passive: was/were + being + V3.
6 All the tickets _____ before we arrived at the box office.
A were sold
B had been sold
C have been sold
D are sold
✓ Past Perfect Passive "had been sold" — tickets sold BEFORE we arrived (earlier of two past events). Formula: S + had + been + V3.
✗ Action completed BEFORE another past action = Past Perfect Passive: S + had + been + V3.
7 The winners _____ at the ceremony next Friday.
A will be announced
B are announced
C were announced
D have been announced
✓ Future Simple Passive "will be announced" — "next Friday" = future. Formula: S + will + be + V3.
✗ "Next Friday" = future = Future Simple Passive: S + will + be + V3.
8 Mobile phones _____ in this examination hall.
A are not allowed to use
B must not be used
C should not use
D cannot used
✓ Modal Passive "must not be used" — rule/prohibition. Formula: S + modal + not + be + V3.
✗ Modal Passive formula: S + modal + be + V3. Note: "cannot used" is wrong — must be "cannot be used."
9 _____ that the project will be delayed due to budget issues.
A People say
B It is said
C They are saying
D It says
✓ Impersonal Passive "It is said" — reporting what people say. Formula: It + is + V3 + that + clause.
✗ Impersonal passive: "It is said/believed/expected/reported + that + clause."
10 The medicine should _____ in a cool, dry place.
A store
B be stored
C be storing
D have stored
✓ Modal Passive "should be stored" — instruction/advice. After a modal verb, always: be + V3 (not be + V-ing).
✗ Modal + passive = modal + be + V3 (never modal + be + V-ing for passive).

Rewrite each sentence in passive voice. Click Reveal to check.

📌 Follow the 4 steps: (1) Move object to subject, (2) Add correct BE, (3) Change verb to V3, (4) Add "by + agent" (or omit).
Active
1. Shakespeare wrote Hamlet.
→ Passive:
Hamlet was written by Shakespeare.
✓ was written by Shakespeare — Past Simple Passive (wrote → was written). "By Shakespeare" is kept as the agent is famous/important.
Active
2. They are building a new shopping mall.
→ Passive:
A new shopping mall is being built.
✓ is being built — Present Continuous Passive. "They" is omitted as it's unimportant/vague.
Active
3. The government will introduce a new law next year.
→ Passive:
A new law will be introduced by the government next year.
✓ will be introduced (by the government) next year — Future Simple Passive. Modal "will" + be + V3.
Active
4. Someone had stolen her bicycle before she noticed.
→ Passive:
Her bicycle had been stolen before she noticed.
✓ had been stolen — Past Perfect Passive. "Someone" is omitted (unknown agent). Formula: had + been + V3.
Active
5. Scientists have discovered a new planet.
→ Passive:
A new planet has been discovered by scientists.
✓ has been discovered (by scientists) — Present Perfect Passive. Formula: has/have + been + V3.
Active
6. You must complete this form in black ink.
→ Passive:
This form must be completed in black ink.
✓ must be completed in black ink — Modal Passive. "You" is omitted (general instruction). Modal + be + V3.
Active
7. People believe that he is innocent.
→ Passive (Impersonal):
It is believed that he is innocent / He is believed to be innocent.
✓ It is believed that he is innocent OR He is believed to be innocent — Both are correct impersonal passive forms.
Active
8. Workers were painting the walls when the manager arrived.
→ Passive:
The walls were being painted when the manager arrived.
✓ were being painted — Past Continuous Passive. "Workers" omitted. Formula: was/were + being + V3.

Error Correction

Each passive sentence has ONE error. Find and fix it.

📌 Click "Show Correction" to see the fix and which rule was broken.
1. The letters was sent by the secretary yesterday. ✗ was
✓ The letters were sent by the secretary yesterday.
"Letters" = plural → were (not was). S–V agreement in passive voice.
2. This bridge is build in 1890. ✗ is build
✓ This bridge was built in 1890.
"In 1890" = specific past time → Past Simple Passive (was/were + V3), not Present Simple. Also: "build" → V3 = "built".
3. The movie is being filmed by was directed by James Cameron. ✗ double verb
✓ The movie is being filmed by James Cameron. OR The movie was directed by James Cameron.
One passive verb per clause. Remove one verb form entirely.
4. She should be promoting to manager. ✗ promoting
✓ She should be promoted to manager.
Modal passive = modal + be + V3 (past participle), NEVER modal + be + V-ing.
5. It is believed that prices will be raise next month. ✗ raise
✓ It is believed that prices will be raised next month.
After "will be", always use V3 (past participle): "raised", not base verb "raise".
6. The package had delivered before I got home. ✗ had delivered
✓ The package had been delivered before I got home.
Past Perfect Passive needs "been": had + been + V3. Cannot skip "been".

Section E — Unit Review

Master summary + self-evaluation checklist.

🚨
Top 8 Exam Traps (ข้อสอบชอบออก):
  1. was wrote ✗ → was written ✓ — always V3 (past participle), never V2
  2. The letters was ✗ → The letters were ✓ — S–V agreement still applies in passive
  3. is being build ✗ → is being built ✓ — V3 after "being", not base verb
  4. had delivered (passive meaning) ✗ → had been delivered ✓ — don't skip "been"
  5. should be wearing (passive) ✗ → should be worn ✓ — modal passive never uses -ing
  6. is been ✗ → has been ✓ — Present Perfect Passive needs "has/have"
  7. will been ✗ → will have been ✓ — Future Perfect Passive needs "have"
  8. It is expected to (no subject) ✗ → It is expected that... ✓ — "It" is required as dummy subject
