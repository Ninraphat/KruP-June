Section B — Reading Passage

A news report mixing direct and reported speech. Identify each type!

🎨
AMBER = Direct Speech  ·  TEAL = Reported Speech

School Wins Regional Science Award

~200 words · News Report · B1

Prasert Secondary School has won the regional science award for the third consecutive year. The principal, Dr Suda, announced that the school's science team had worked incredibly hard all year. She added that the students deserved all the credit for their dedication.

Team leader Noon told reporters, "We have been preparing for this competition for six months." She explained that they had conducted over forty experiments before choosing their final project.

Science teacher Mr Dang said that he had never been so proud of a group of students. He mentioned that the team would represent the school at the national level next month.

When asked about the secret to their success, Noon replied, "You have to believe in yourself and never give up." Mr Dang added that students who worked together always achieved more than those who worked alone.

The regional judge commented that this had been the strongest year of competition he had ever seen.

Q

Reading Comprehension

Click to check.

1 "She announced that the school's science team had worked incredibly hard all year" — why is "had worked" used instead of "have worked"?
A Because the action happened before the announcement
B Tense backshift: Present Perfect ("have worked") shifts to Past Perfect ("had worked") in reported speech
C Because "had" is always used in reported speech
D Because "have worked" is incorrect English
✓ Tense backshift: when the reporting verb is past ("announced"), verbs in the reported clause shift one tense back. Present Perfect ("have worked") → Past Perfect ("had worked").
✗ This is tense backshift. Past reporting verb → everything shifts one tense back.
2 Noon said "We have been preparing for six months." In reported speech, this becomes:
A She said they have been preparing for six months.
B She said they were preparing for six months.
C She said they had been preparing for six months.
D She said they are preparing for six months.
✓ Present Perfect Continuous ("have been preparing") → Past Perfect Continuous ("had been preparing") after a past reporting verb. Also: "we" → "they".
✗ Present Perfect Continuous shifts to Past Perfect Continuous in reported speech.
3 Mr Dang "mentioned that the team would represent the school next month." Why is "would" used (not "will")?
A Tense backshift: "will" shifts to "would" in reported speech after a past reporting verb
B Because "would" is always used in reported speech
C Because the event didn't happen
D Because "will" is incorrect here
✓ "Will" → "would" is one of the standard tense backshifts. After past reporting verbs like "mentioned", "will" becomes "would".
✗ "Will" shifts to "would" in reported speech after past reporting verbs.

Section C — Grammar Spotlight: Reported Statements

Tense backshift, pronoun changes, and time expression changes.

🔧
What is Reported Speech?
Reported (indirect) speech tells us what someone said without using their exact words. When the reporting verb is in the past (said, told, asked…), the verbs inside shift one tense back — this is called tense backshift.
1

4

SAY vs. TELL

The most common reporting verb pair — and the most confused!

›
SAY

say (that) — no person object directly after "say"

✓ She said she was tired.
✓ He said (that) it was raining.
✗ She said me she was tired.

TELL

tell + person — ALWAYS needs a person object

✓ She told me she was tired.
✓ He told us it was raining.
✗ She told she was tired.

❌
Most common error: "She said me that she was tired." ✗
"Say" NEVER takes a person object directly. Use "told me" or "said (that)":
→ "She told me she was tired." ✓ / "She said she was tired." ✓

Section D — Reported Questions

How to report Yes/No questions and Wh- questions correctly.

🔧
Key rule for ALL reported questions:
1. Change the word order back to normal (Subject + Verb, NOT question order)
2. Remove the auxiliary "do/does/did"
3. Apply tense backshift (same as reported statements)
4. Do NOT use a question mark (?)
Y/N

Yes/No Questions — use IF or WHETHER

ถ้าไม่มีคำถามเพื่อนำคำตอบ ใช้ if หรือ whether

›
🔧
Formula: asked + if/whether + Subject + Verb (normal order, no question mark)
Direct Question "Are you ready?"
"Do you like coffee?"
"Have you finished?"
"Will you come?"
Reported Question He asked if I was ready.
She asked if I liked coffee.
He asked if I had finished.
She asked if I would come.
❌
Error: "He asked if was I ready?" ✗
→ "He asked if I was ready." ✓ (normal word order, no question mark)
Wh-

Wh- Questions — keep the question word

คำถาม Wh- เก็บไว้ แต่เปลี่ยนลำดับคำเB8;์นปกติ

›
🔧
Formula: asked + Wh-word + Subject + Verb (normal order, no question mark)
Direct Question "Where do you live?"
"What did she say?"
"Why are you late?"
"When will they arrive?"
"Who is he?"
Reported Question He asked where I lived.
She asked what she had said.
He asked why I was late.
She asked when they would arrive.
He asked who he was.
❌
Error: "She asked where did I live?" ✗
→ "She asked where I lived." ✓ (remove "did", normal word order, no question mark)
CMD

Reported Commands & Requests

คำสั่งและคำขอร้อง ใช้ to + V1 หรือ not to + V1

›
🔧
Positive command: told/asked/ordered + person + to + V1
Negative command: told/asked/warned + person + not to + V1
Direct Command "Open the window!"
"Please be quiet."
"Don't touch that!"
"Be careful!"
Reported Command He told me to open the window.
She asked us to be quiet.
He told me not to touch that.
She warned me to be careful.
🔧

Section E — Reporting Verbs & Patterns

Different verbs need different grammar patterns after them.

PatternVerbsExample
verb + that + clausesay, tell, explain, announce, admit, claim, deny, insist, suggest, warn, promiseHe said that he was tired. / She admitted that she had lied.
verb + to + V1promise, offer, refuse, agree, threaten, decide, ask (for self)She promised to return it. / He refused to answer.
verb + person + to + V1tell, ask, order, warn, remind, advise, persuade, inviteShe told me to sit down. / He warned her not to go.
verb + V-ingadmit, deny, suggest, recommend, avoid, regret, mentionHe admitted stealing it. / She suggested going out.
verb + person + of + V-ingaccuse, remind (of)She accused him of lying. / He reminded her of calling them.
verb + preposition + V-inginsist on, apologise for, object to, complain aboutHe insisted on paying. / She apologised for being late.
⚡
Key Reporting Verbs — Know These for Exams:
deny + V-ing: "I didn't do it!" → He denied doing it.
suggest + V-ing or that + S + should: "Let's go!" → She suggested going / She suggested that they should go.
warn + person + not to: "Don't go out!" → He warned me not to go out.
promise + to V1: "I'll help you." → He promised to help me.
accuse + of + V-ing: "You stole it!" → She accused him of stealing it.

Section F — Exercises

Five activities covering all aspects of reported speech.

1

Identify & Choose

Choose the correct reported speech form.

📌 Apply tense backshift and change pronouns correctly.
1 Direct: "I am very happy today." She said _____ very happy that day.
A she is
B she was
C I am
D she has been
✓ "am" (Present Simple) → "was" (Past Simple). Pronoun "I" → "she". Time word "today" → "that day".
✗ Present Simple backshifts to Past Simple. "I" → "she". "Today" → "that day".
2 Direct: "I have already eaten." He said he _____ already eaten.
A has
B had
C have
D was
✓ Present Perfect ("have eaten") → Past Perfect ("had eaten"). "I" → "he".
✗ Present Perfect backshifts to Past Perfect: "have/has" → "had".
3 Direct: "Will you help me?" She asked him if he _____ help her.
A will
B would
C can
D could
✓ "will" → "would" (backshift). Yes/No question → use "if". Normal word order: "if he would help her."
✗ "Will" backshifts to "would". Yes/No question → "if/whether + subject + verb".
4 Direct: "Don't use your phone in class!" The teacher told us _____ our phones in class.
A don't use
B to not use
C not to use
D to use not
✓ Negative command → told + person + NOT TO + V1. "not to use" is the correct form.
✗ Negative reported command: told + person + NOT TO + V1 (never "to not use").
5 Direct: "I was watching TV when you called." She said she _____ TV when I had called.
A was watching
B had been watching
C has been watching
D would be watching
✓ Past Continuous ("was watching") → Past Perfect Continuous ("had been watching"). "I" → "she", "you" → "I".
✗ Past Continuous backshifts to Past Perfect Continuous.
6 Direct: "Where do you live?" She asked me where I _____.
A did live
B do live
C lived
D was living
✓ Wh- question: remove "do", normal word order, backshift. "do live" → "lived". No question mark.
✗ Wh- reported question: keep the Wh- word + normal word order + tense backshift. Remove "do/does/did".
7 He said, "I can't come tomorrow." He said he _____ come the next day.
A can't
B couldn't
C won't
D wouldn't
✓ "can't" → "couldn't" (backshift). "tomorrow" → "the next day".
✗ "can" → "could" in reported speech. "Can't" → "couldn't". "Tomorrow" → "the next day".
8 "Let's go to the cinema tonight," she said. She suggested _____ to the cinema that night.
A to go
B they go
C going
D that go
✓ "suggest" + V-ing (gerund). "Tonight" → "that night".
✗ "Suggest" is followed by V-ing (gerund) or "that + subject + should". NOT "to + V1".
2

Convert to Reported Speech

Rewrite each sentence in reported speech.

📌 Click Reveal to check your answer and rule.
1. Direct: "I am going to London next week."
She said she was going to London the following week.
✓ she was going to London the following week — "am going" → "was going"; "I" → "she"; "next week" → "the following week".
2. Direct: "Have you finished the report?"
She asked me if I had finished the report.
✓ I had finished the report — Y/N question → "if"; Present Perfect → Past Perfect; normal word order; no question mark.
3. Direct: "Why didn't you call me yesterday?"
He asked me why I hadn't called him the day before.
✓ I hadn't called him the day before — "didn't call" → "hadn't called"; "me" → "him"; "yesterday" → "the day before"; normal word order.
4. Direct: "Please wait outside."
She asked us to wait outside.
✓ to wait outside — polite request → asked + person + to + V1.
5. Direct: "I will never lie to you again."
He promised never to lie to her again / that he would never lie to her again.
✓ never to lie to her again OR that he would never lie to her again — "promise" + to + V1 or "promise + that + clause"; "will" → "would"; "you" → "her".
6. Direct: "I didn't break the vase!"
He denied breaking the vase / having broken the vase.
✓ breaking the vase (or "having broken the vase") — "deny" is always followed by V-ing (gerund).
7. Direct: "What time does the train leave?"
She asked what time the train left.
✓ the train left — Wh- question: keep "what time", remove "does", normal word order, backshift (Present Simple → Past Simple).
8. Direct: "You should see a doctor," she said.
She advised me to see a doctor.
✓ to see a doctor — "advise" + person + to + V1. (Also possible: "she said I should see a doctor.")
3

Error Correction

Each sentence has ONE reported speech error. Find and fix it.

📌 Click "Show Correction" to see the fix and rule.
1. She said me that she was tired. ✗ said me
✓ She told me that she was tired. (or: She said that she was tired.)
"Say" never takes a person directly after it. Use "tell + person" or "say (that)".
2. He asked me where did I live? ✗ did I live?
✓ He asked me where I lived.
Reported questions: normal word order (not question order), remove "did", tense backshift, NO question mark.
3. She told me to not go there alone. ✗ to not go
✓ She told me not to go there alone.
Negative reported command: "not to + V1" (NOT "to not + V1"). "Not" comes BEFORE "to".
4. He suggested to go to the park. ✗ to go
✓ He suggested going to the park.
"Suggest" is NEVER followed by "to + V1". Use V-ing (gerund) or "that + subject + should".
5. She asked whether I will join them. ✗ will
✓ She asked whether I would join them.
Tense backshift: "will" → "would" in reported speech after a past reporting verb.
6. He admitted to steal the money. ✗ to steal
✓ He admitted stealing the money.
"Admit" is followed by V-ing (gerund), NOT "to + V1".
4

Choose the Correct Reporting Verb

Select the verb that fits the meaning and structure.

📌 Read the direct speech and choose the best reporting verb.
1 "I definitely didn't take your phone!" (strong denial)
A He said not taking my phone.
B He denied taking my phone.
C He refused taking my phone.
D He suggested taking my phone.
✓ "deny" = to say you did not do something. "Deny" + V-ing → "He denied taking my phone."
✗ "Deny" means to say something is not true. Pattern: deny + V-ing.
2 "Yes, I did break the window. I'm sorry." (confession)
A She admitted breaking the window.
B She denied breaking the window.
C She warned breaking the window.
D She refused to break the window.
✓ "admit" = to say you did something wrong. "Admit" + V-ing → "She admitted breaking the window."
✗ Confessing to doing something wrong = "admit" + V-ing.
3 "Watch out! The road is icy!" (danger warning)
A He told me that the road was icy.
B He warned me that the road was icy.
C He asked me that the road was icy.
D He suggested that the road was icy.
✓ "warn" = to alert someone to danger. "He warned me that the road was icy."
✗ Alerting someone to danger = "warn". Pattern: warn + person + that / warn + person + not to.
4 "Why don't we visit the museum?" (suggestion)
A She asked visiting the museum.
B She suggested visiting the museum.
C She promised to visit the museum.
D She warned visiting the museum.
✓ "suggest" = to put forward an idea. "Suggest" + V-ing → "She suggested visiting the museum."
✗ Making a suggestion = "suggest" + V-ing (gerund).
5 "You cheated on the exam!" he said to her. (accusation)
A He told her of cheating on the exam.
B He accused her of cheating on the exam.
C He warned her of cheating on the exam.
D He admitted her cheating on the exam.
✓ "accuse" = to say someone has done something wrong. Pattern: accuse + person + of + V-ing.
✗ Accusing someone of wrongdoing = "accuse + person + of + V-ing".

Section G — Unit Review

Complete summary + self-evaluation checklist.

🚨
Top 8 Exam Traps:
  1. "She said me that she was tired." ✗ → "She told me" or "She said that she was tired." ✓
  2. Forgetting tense backshift: "He said he is tired." ✗ → "He said he was tired." ✓
  3. Question word order in reported questions: "He asked where was I." ✗ → "He asked where I was." ✓
  4. Using question mark in reported speech: "She asked if I was ready?" ✗ → no "?" in reported speech
  5. "not to" vs "to not": "She told me to not go." ✗ → "She told me not to go." ✓
  6. "suggest + to V1": "He suggested to go." ✗ → "He suggested going." ✓
  7. "admit/deny + to V1": "He denied to take it." ✗ → "He denied taking it." ✓
  8. "must" backshift: "He said he must go." (wrong in past context) → "He said he had to go." ✓

Self-Evaluation Checklist — "I Can…"

  • Apply all tense backshifts correctly in reported statements.
  • Change pronouns and possessives correctly.
  • Convert time expressions (today → that day, tomorrow → the next day, etc.).
  • Use "say" vs "tell" correctly (tell + person; say + that).
  • Report Yes/No questions using "if/whether + S + V" (normal order, no "?").
  • Report Wh- questions (keep Wh-word, normal order, remove do/does/did).
  • Report commands using "to + V1" or "not to + V1".
  • Use the correct reporting verb for each speech act (suggest, deny, admit, warn, accuse, promise).
  • Know that scientific facts don't require backshift.
  • Score at least 12/14 on the exam-style MCQ section.
