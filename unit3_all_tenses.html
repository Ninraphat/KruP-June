Section B — Reading Passage

A diary entry using 8 different tenses. Find the tense clues!

My Trip to Japan — A Travel Diary

I arrived in Tokyo three days ago, and I have already visited five temples! Right now, I am sitting in a small café, writing this diary while it is raining outside.

Yesterday, while I was walking through Shibuya, I met a group of students from Korea. By the time we had finished talking, it was already dark!

Tomorrow, I will visit Kyoto. At this time tomorrow, I will be exploring the famous bamboo forest. By the end of this trip, I will have travelled to four cities and will have taken over 500 photos!

Q

Reading Comprehension

Click an option to check your answer.

1 Which tense is used in "I have already visited five temples"?
A Past Simple
B Present Perfect
C Present Continuous
D Future Perfect
✅ "have + already + V.3 (visited)" = Present Perfect — an action completed before now, with a result that matters now.
❌ Look for "have/has + V.3" — that's the Present Perfect structure.
2 "By the time we had finished talking, it was already dark" — why is "had finished" used?
A It describes an action happening now.
B It describes an action completed BEFORE another past action (it was dark).
C It describes a future plan.
D It describes a habitual action.
✅ Past Perfect (had + V.3) shows one past action finished BEFORE another past action started — "finished talking" happened before "it was dark."
❌ Past Perfect always shows the EARLIER of two past events.
3 "At this time tomorrow, I will be exploring the bamboo forest" — what tense is this?
A Future Simple
B Future Continuous
C Future Perfect
D Present Continuous
✅ "will be + V-ing" = Future Continuous — an action that will be IN PROGRESS at a specific future time ("at this time tomorrow").
❌ "will be + V-ing" is the Future Continuous formula.

🔵 PRESENT TENSES

1

Present Simple

กริยาปัจจุบันธรรมดา

›
S + V1 (+s/es) negative: S + do/does not + V1 question: Do/Does + S + V1?
Use 1 — Facts & General TruthsThe sun rises in the east. ความจริงทั่วไปที่ไม่เปลี่ยนแปลง
Use 2 — Habits & RoutinesShe brushes her teeth every morning. การกระทำที่ทำเป็นประจำ
Use 3 — Schedules & TimetablesThe train leaves at 9 AM. ตารางเวลาที่กำหนดไว้แน่นอน
every day always usually often sometimes never on Mondays
❌
Common Error: "She go to school every day." → She goes to school. (3rd person singular: He/She/It + V-s)
2

Present Continuous

กริยาปัจจุบันต่อเนื่อง / กำลังเกิดขึ้น

›
S + am/is/are + V-ing negative: S + am/is/are + not + V-ing question: Am/Is/Are + S + V-ing?
Use 1 — Action happening nowShe is reading a book right now. การกระทำที่กำลังเกิดขึ้นในขณะพูด
Use 2 — Temporary situationsI am staying with my aunt this month. สถานการณ์ชั่วคราว
Use 3 — Fixed future arrangementsWe are meeting them tomorrow. แผนการที่นัดไว้แล้วในอนาคต
now right now at the moment currently Look! Listen!
⚠️
Stative verbs (know, like, love, want, believe, understand, own, belong) are usually NOT used in continuous form. "I am knowing the answer" ✗ → "I know the answer" ✓
3

Present Perfect

กริยาปัจจุบันสมบูรณ์

›
S + have/has + V3 negative: S + have/has + not + V3 question: Have/Has + S + V3?
Use 1 — Experience (unspecified time)I have visited Japan twice. ประสบการณ์ที่เคยทำ ไม่ระบุเวลาที่แน่นอน
Use 2 — Action started in past, continues to nowShe has lived here since 2018. เริ่มในอดีตและต่อเนื่องถึงปัจจุบัน
Use 3 — Recently completed action with present resultHe has just finished his homework. การกระทำที่เพิ่งเสร็จและมีผลต่อปัจจุบัน
already yet just ever never since for so far
❌
Common Error: "I have went to Paris last year." → "last year" = specific past time → use Past Simple: "I went to Paris last year."
4

Present Perfect Continuous

กริยาปัจจุบันสมบูรณ์ต่อเนื่อง

›
S + have/has + been + V-ing negative: S + have/has + not been + V-ing
Use 1 — Duration of an ongoing actionI have been waiting for an hour. การกระทำที่ต่อเนื่องมาจนถึงปัจจุบัน เน้นช่วงเวลา
Use 2 — Recent activity with visible resultHer eyes are red — she has been crying. การกระทำที่ผ่านมาเร็วๆนี้ ส่งผลถึงปัจจุบัน
all day for hours lately recently since morning
⚖️
Present Perfect vs. Present Perfect Continuous: "I have read 3 books" (focus on RESULT/quantity) vs. "I have been reading all afternoon" (focus on DURATION/process).

🟠 PAST TENSES

5

Past Simple

กริยาอดีตธรรมดา

›
S + V2 (regular: V+ed) negative: S + did not + V1 question: Did + S + V1?
Use 1 — Completed action at specific past timeShe visited Korea last summer. เหตุการณ์ที่เกิดขึ้นและจบลงแล้วในอดีต
Use 2 — Series of completed past actionsHe woke up, ate breakfast, and left. ลำดับเหตุการณ์ในอดีต
Use 3 — Past habits (with "used to")I used to play football every weekend. นิสัยในอดีตที่ไม่ทำแล้ว
yesterday last week/month/year ago in 2020 when I was young
❌
Common Error: "She did went to school." → "did" already shows past — use base verb: "She did not go / Did she go?"
6

Past Continuous

กริยาอดีตต่อเนื่อง / กำลังเกิดขึ้นในอดีต

›
S + was/were + V-ing negative: S + was/were + not + V-ing
Use 1 — Action in progress at a specific past timeAt 8 PM yesterday, I was studying. การกระทำที่กำลังดำเนินอยู่ ณ เวลาหนึ่งในอดีต
Use 2 — Interrupted action (with Past Simple)I was sleeping when the phone rang. การกระทำที่กำลังเกิดถูกขัดจังหวะโดยอีกการกระทำ
Use 3 — Two simultaneous past actionsWhile she was cooking, he was cleaning. สองเหตุการณ์เกิดพร้อมกันในอดีต
while when (+ Past Simple) at that moment at 8 PM yesterday all day yesterday
🔑
Key pattern: WHILE + Past Continuous (longer action), WHEN + Past Simple (shorter, interrupting action). "While I was walking home, it started to rain."
7

Past Perfect

กริยาอดีตสมบูรณ์

›
S + had + V3 negative: S + had + not + V3 question: Had + S + V3?
Use — Action completed BEFORE another past actionBy the time I arrived, the meeting had already started. การกระทำที่เกิดขึ้นและจบลงก่อนอีกเหตุการณ์ในอดีต
Use — In reported speech (backshift from Present Perfect)She said she had finished the report. (original: "I have finished")
before after by the time already (in past context) by 6 PM
🔑
Two past events, two tenses: The EARLIER event = Past Perfect (had + V3). The LATER event = Past Simple. "The train had already left [earlier] when we arrived [later] at the station."

🟢 FUTURE TENSES

8

Future Simple

กริยาอนาคตธรรมดา

›
S + will + V1 negative: S + will not (won't) + V1 question: Will + S + V1?
Use 1 — Prediction (no evidence/spontaneous decision)I think it will rain tomorrow. การคาดการณ์ / การตัดสินใจทันที
Use 2 — Promise / OfferI will help you with your homework. คำสัญญา / การเสนอตัว
⚖️
will vs. be going to: "Will" = spontaneous decision/general prediction. "Be going to" = plan made before now, or prediction WITH evidence ("Look at those clouds — it's going to rain.")
tomorrow next week/month/year soon in the future
9

Future Continuous

กริยาอนาคตต่อเนื่อง

›
S + will be + V-ing negative: S + will not be + V-ing
Use — Action in progress at a specific future timeAt 10 AM tomorrow, I will be taking my exam. การกระทำที่กำลังดำเนินอยู่ ณ เวลาหนึ่งในอนาคต
at this time tomorrow at 8 PM next Friday this time next year
🔑
Pattern parallels Past Continuous, but moved to the future: "At this time tomorrow, I will be flying to Japan" — picture a specific moment with the action already in progress.
10

Future Perfect

กริยาอนาคตสมบูรณ์

›
S + will have + V3 negative: S + will not have + V3
Use — Action completed BEFORE a specific future timeBy 2030, she will have graduated from university. การกระทำที่จะเสร็จสิ้นภายในเวลาหนึ่งในอนาคต
by + future time by the end of by next year
🔑
"BY" is the key signal word. "By next month, I will have finished this project." = the action will be complete at or before that future point.
11

Future Perfect Continuous

กริยาอนาคตสมบูรณ์ต่อเนื่อง

›
S + will have been + V-ing
Use — Duration of action up to a future pointBy June, I will have been working here for 5 years. ระยะเวลาที่กระทำต่อเนื่องจนถึงจุดหนึ่งในอนาคต
by + time + for + duration for X years by then
📝
This is the least common tense — focus on RECOGNIZING it rather than producing it. Pattern: "By [future time], [subject] will have been [V-ing] for [duration]."

⭐ BONUS: USED TO vs. BE USED TO

12

Used to / Be used to / Get used to

เคย (อดีต) / ชินกับ (ปัจจุบัน)

›
FormMeaningFollowed byExample
used to + V1เคยทำ (แต่ไม่ทำแล้ว) — past habitbase verbI used to play tennis. (now I don't)
be used to + V-ing/nounชินกับ — accustomed togerund / nounI am used to waking up early. (it's normal for me)
get used to + V-ing/nounกำลังจะชิน — becoming accustomedgerund / nounShe is getting used to living abroad.
❌
Common Error: "I am used to wake up early." → must use gerund (V-ing) after "be/get used to": "I am used to waking up early."

Section D — Exercises

Five activities covering all 12 tenses — from identification to full exam practice.

1

Identify the Tense

Read each sentence and choose which tense is used.

📌 Look at the verb form and time words to identify the tense.
1 "By the time the movie started, we had already eaten dinner."
A Past Simple
B Past Perfect
C Present Perfect
D Past Continuous
✅ "had + V3 (eaten)" = Past Perfect — eating dinner happened BEFORE the movie started (another past event).
❌ "had + V3" is the formula for Past Perfect — an earlier past action.
2 "She has been working on this project for three months."
A Present Perfect
B Present Perfect Continuous
C Present Continuous
D Past Perfect Continuous
✅ "has been + V-ing (working)" = Present Perfect Continuous — emphasises the ongoing duration ("for three months").
❌ "has been + V-ing" = Present Perfect Continuous, focuses on duration of an ongoing action.
3 "At this time next week, I will be lying on a beach in Phuket."
A Future Simple
B Future Continuous
C Future Perfect
D Present Continuous
✅ "will be + V-ing" = Future Continuous. "At this time next week" signals an action in progress at a specific future moment.
❌ "will be + V-ing" = Future Continuous, used with "at this time + future time."
4 "I used to live in Bangkok, but now I live in Chiang Mai."
A Past habit (used to)
B Present habit
C Be used to (accustomed)
D Future plan
✅ "used to + V1 (live)" = a habit/situation that existed in the past but no longer does. "but now I live..." confirms the contrast.
❌ "used to + base verb" describes something that was true in the past but isn't anymore.
5 "By 2030, scientists will have developed a cure for this disease."
A Future Simple
B Future Continuous
C Future Perfect
D Present Perfect
✅ "will have + V3 (developed)" = Future Perfect — an action that will be COMPLETE by a specific future point ("by 2030").
❌ "will have + V3" = Future Perfect, signalled by "by + future time."
6 "While she was cooking dinner, the phone rang."
A Past Simple only
B Past Continuous + Past Simple
C Present Continuous + Past Simple
D Past Perfect + Past Simple
✅ "was cooking" (Past Continuous = longer ongoing action) is interrupted by "rang" (Past Simple = shorter sudden action). Classic "while...when" pattern.
❌ "was/were + V-ing" = Past Continuous (the longer action), interrupted by a Past Simple verb.
2

Fill in the Correct Tense Form

Conjugate the verb in brackets correctly.

📌 Click Reveal to see the answer and which tense rule applies.
1. By the time we (arrive) at the cinema, the film had already started.
✅ had already started — Past Perfect: an action completed BEFORE another past action (we arrived).
2. Look! It is raining (rain) heavily outside.
✅ is raining — Present Continuous: action happening right now, signalled by "Look!"
3. My grandmother has lived (live) in this house for 40 years.
✅ has lived — Present Perfect with "for" — action started in the past and continues now.
4. This time tomorrow, I will be taking (take) my final exam.
✅ will be taking — Future Continuous: action in progress at a specific future time.
5. When I was a child, I used to play (play) outside every day after school.
✅ used to play — past habit that is no longer true.
6. I'm exhausted! I have been studying (study) since 7 AM.
✅ have been studying — Present Perfect Continuous: emphasises duration up to now ("since 7 AM"), with present result (exhaustion).
7. While I was walking (walk) to school, I saw an accident.
✅ was walking — Past Continuous: the longer ongoing action, interrupted by "saw" (Past Simple).
8. By the end of this year, she will have saved (save) enough money for a car.

3

Error Correction

Each sentence has one tense error. Find and fix it.

📌 Click "Show Correction" to see the fix and explanation.
1. I have went to the dentist yesterday. ❌ have went
✅ I went to the dentist yesterday.
"Yesterday" = specific past time → Past Simple, not Present Perfect.
2. She is knowing the answer to that question. ❌ is knowing
✅ She knows the answer to that question.
"Know" is a stative verb — never used in continuous form.
3. By the time the ambulance arrived, the patient already died. ❌ already died
✅ By the time the ambulance arrived, the patient had already died.
"By the time" signals Past Perfect — the death happened BEFORE the ambulance arrived (another past event).
4. I am used to wake up early every morning. ❌ wake
✅ I am used to waking up early every morning.
"be used to" must be followed by a gerund (V-ing), not the base verb.
5. Tomorrow at 8 AM, I am flying to Tokyo (fixed plan), so right now I am being very excited. ❌ am being
✅ ...so right now I am very excited.
"Be" describing an emotion/state is stative — don't use continuous form ("am being") unless describing temporary behaviour.
6. Did you ever visit Europe? ❌ Did you ever visit
✅ Have you ever visited Europe?
"Ever" (asking about life experience, unspecified time) signals Present Perfect, not Past Simple.

Exam-Style Multiple Choice (12 Questions)

Full exam format — matches ม.1 Advanced / EP difficulty.

📌 Choose the best verb form for each sentence.
1 By the time you read this letter, I _____ for New York.
A leave
B will leave
C will have left
D have left
✅ "By the time" + future action = Future Perfect — by the time of reading, the leaving will already be complete.
❌ "By the time" with a future action signals Future Perfect: will have + V3.
2 My brother _____ basketball every Saturday morning.
A plays
B is playing
C played
D has played
✅ "Every Saturday morning" = routine → Present Simple. Note 3rd person singular "plays".
❌ "Every Saturday morning" signals a routine — Present Simple.
3 I'm so tired because I _____ all night for the exam.
A study
B studied
C have been studying
D will study
✅ Present Perfect Continuous — duration ("all night") leading to a present result ("I'm so tired").
❌ "All night" + present result (tired now) = Present Perfect Continuous.
4 When I got home, my dog _____ on the sofa.
A sleeps
B was sleeping
C has slept
D will sleep
✅ Past Continuous — the dog was already sleeping (ongoing) when I arrived (interrupting Past Simple action).
❌ "When + Past Simple" interrupts an ongoing Past Continuous action.
5 _____ you ever _____ sushi before?
A Did / eat
B Have / eaten
C Do / eat
D Are / eating
✅ "Ever" + life experience (unspecified time) = Present Perfect → "Have you ever eaten...?"
❌ "Ever" asking about life experience always pairs with Present Perfect.
6 Don't call me at 9 PM tonight — I _____ dinner with my family.
A will eat
B will be eating
C eat
D have eaten
✅ Future Continuous — an action that will be in progress at a specific future time (9 PM tonight).
❌ A specific future time + ongoing action = Future Continuous (will be + V-ing).
7 She told me that she _____ the movie twice already.
A sees
B has seen
C had seen
D will see
✅ In reported speech, Present Perfect ("has seen") shifts back to Past Perfect ("had seen") when the main verb is past ("told").
❌ Reported speech with past main verb ("told") shifts Present Perfect → Past Perfect.
8 Water _____ at 100 degrees Celsius.
A boils
B is boiling
C boiled
D has boiled
✅ Scientific fact = Present Simple, regardless of when you're speaking.
❌ Universal/scientific facts always use Present Simple.
9 I can't go out tonight — I _____ my room since this morning, and it's still a mess.
A clean
B cleaned
C have been cleaning
D will clean
✅ "Since this morning" + ongoing unfinished action = Present Perfect Continuous.
❌ "Since" + an action still ongoing = Present Perfect Continuous.
10 In 10 years, robots _____ most factory jobs.
A do
B will do
C have done
D are doing
✅ "In 10 years" = future prediction without "by" → Future Simple (will + V1), not Future Perfect.
❌ "In [time period]" without "by" → simple future prediction = will + V1.
11 He moved to Bangkok last year. Since then, he _____ three different jobs.
A had
B has had
C has
D will have
✅ "Since then" connects past to present = Present Perfect — the period from moving until now.
❌ "Since then" = Present Perfect, linking a past starting point to now.
12 I'm not used to _____ in such a noisy environment.
A work
B working
C worked
D to work
✅ "be used to" + gerund (V-ing). "Not used to working" = not accustomed to working.
❌ "(not) used to" + V-ing (gerund form), never the base verb.

Section E — Unit Review

Master summary of all 12 tenses + self-evaluation checklist.

🚨
Top 8 Exam Traps (ข้อสอบชอบออก):
  1. have went ✗ → went ✓ or have gone ✓ — never mix "have" + V2
  2. since yesterday with Past Simple ✗ → "since" always pairs with Present Perfect
  3. am knowing / am understanding ✗ → stative verbs never take -ing
  4. by the time + Past Simple (both clauses) — usually needs Past Perfect in one clause
  5. used to + V-ing ✗ → "used to" + base verb (V1) only
  6. be used to + V1 ✗ → "be used to" + V-ing/noun
  7. will + V1 for "by [future time]" ✗ → use Future Perfect "will have + V3"
  8. ago with Present Perfect ✗ → "ago" always pairs with Past Simple

Self-Evaluation Checklist — "I Can…"

  • Form all 12 tenses correctly (positive, negative, question).
  • Identify the correct tense from time-word clues.
  • Distinguish Present Perfect from Past Simple (since/for vs. ago/yesterday).
  • Use Past Perfect correctly when two past events are mentioned.
  • Use the "while + Past Continuous, Past Simple" pattern correctly.
  • Distinguish Future Simple, Future Continuous, and Future Perfect.
  • Use "used to" vs. "be used to" vs. "get used to" correctly.
  • Avoid using stative verbs in continuous form.
  • Complete a 12-blank mixed-tense cloze passage.
